# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Lab operators, instructors, and students working inside an isolated Proxmox simulation lab. They need a quick way to inspect CNC state, query bot counts, send approved lab commands, and verify target mappings without dropping into a manual telnet flow.

## Product Purpose

Provide a single dashboard for the Mirai simulation lab that exposes CNC status, bot distribution, peer visibility, and preset lab commands. Success means the operator can understand the lab state and trigger the expected control actions from one screen.

## Brand Personality

Retro, operational, and explicit. The interface should feel like a practical early-2000s desktop tool with a hacker-console edge, not a modern SaaS surface. The tone is instructional and confident, with enough visual theater to fit a classroom demo.

## Anti-references

Modern glassmorphism dashboards, generic AI SaaS layouts, soft pastel marketing pages, heavy gradient branding, playful rounded app shells, and any interface that hides task state behind decorative polish.

## Design Principles

1. Make CNC and bot state immediately legible.
2. Use familiar desktop and terminal metaphors instead of invented controls.
3. Keep lab actions grouped, direct, and easy to confirm.
4. Give operational logs and topology the same visual weight as the counters.
5. Preserve a dense, task-first layout that works for projector demos and quick lab checks.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

High contrast for status and text, keyboard-accessible controls, readable labels, and no reliance on color alone for critical state. Motion should stay light and non-essential, with reduced-motion support. The interface should remain usable on smaller screens even though desktop is the primary target.
